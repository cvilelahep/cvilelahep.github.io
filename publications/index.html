2021

  1. Deep Underground Neutrino Experiment (DUNE) Near Detector Conceptual Design Report
    Adam Abed Abud and others
    Instruments, Mar 2021
    \footnotesize \\ – I developed a novel method, described in this report, to estimate the impact of variations in the neutrino-nucleus interaction model on the DUNE Physics performance, using \emphmachine learning techniques. The studies I performed using this method \emphdirectly led to the \emphrecommendation by the project oversight committee and \emphadoption by the collaboration of the innovative DUNE-PRISM movable near detector concept. Largely because of my work, the movable near detector was deemed a \emphcritical “day-one” component of the DUNE strategy to discover neutrino CP violation.
    DOI
  2. Improved constraints on neutrino mixing from the T2K experiment with \mathbf3.13\times10^21 protons on target
    K. Abe and others
    Phys. Rev. D, Mar 2021
    \footnotesize \\ – I was the paper commitee member responsible for Super-Kamiokande section of paper describing significant improvements in event reconstruction and selection since previous long-format T2K publication.
    DOI

2020

  1. Deep Underground Neutrino Experiment (DUNE), Far Detector Technical Design Report, Volume II DUNE Physics
    Babak Abi and others
    Mar 2020
    \footnotesize \\ – The novel method I developed to study the impact of mis-modeling neutrino-nucleus interactions on the DUNE oscillation analysis enabled the \emphquantification of the impact of the DUNE near detector on the experiment’s Physics reach and \emphvalidate its scientific approach.
    arXiv
  2. Constraint on the matter-antimatter symmetry-violating phase in neutrino oscillations
    K. Abe and others
    Nature, Mar 2020
    [Erratum: Nature 583, E16 (2020)]\\ \footnotesize – This publication is a \emphmajor milestone in the path towards the discovery of neutrino CP violation as it describes the first constraint on the CP-violating phase in neutrino oscillations at a confidence level of three standard deviations. This result was possible due to a greatly \emphimproved sample selection at the T2K far detector using new reconstruction methods. I had a \emphcentral role in the development and implementation of the new methods, performing part of the data analysis, all sample preparation and helping to supervise one of the students leading the effort.
    DOI arXiv

2019

  1. Atmospheric neutrino oscillation analysis with improved event reconstruction in Super-Kamiokande IV
    M. Jiang and others
    Prog. Theor. Exp. Phys., Mar 2019
    \footnotesize \\ – This publication presents the \emphfirst use of a modern event reconstruction algorithm in the analysis of Super-Kamiokande atmospheric neutrino data. As the lead developer of the algorithm, I was assigned to the paper editing committee. I reviewed both the analysis and the manuscript ahead of its submission to the journal.
    DOI arXiv
  2. Measurement of the neutrino-oxygen neutral-current quasielastic cross section using atmospheric neutrinos at Super-Kamiokande
    L. Wan and others
    Phys. Rev., Mar 2019
    \footnotesize \\ – While this publication falls somewhat outside of my main research interests, it is of high significance to Super-Kamiokande, describing a measurement of a process which is a background to the search for the diffuse supernova neutrino flux, one of the main near-term goals of the Super-Kamiokande Collaboration. I was assigned to the paper editing committee as an atmospheric neutrino expert. During the review I identified a significant shortcoming in the analysis related to the treatment of \emphneutrino-nucleus interaction model uncertainties. I suggested a method to estimate these uncertainties and a change to the event selection criteria that made the analysis substantially more \emphrobust. Both my suggestions were implemented and significantly improved the quality of the analysis, which sailed through the \emphPhys. Rev. D peer-review process.
    DOI arXiv

2018

  1. Search for CP Violation in Neutrino and Antineutrino Oscillations by the T2K Experiment with 2.2\times 10^21 Protons on Target
    K. Abe and others
    Phys. Rev. Lett., Mar 2018
    \footnotesize \\ – This publication describes the first measurement of neutrino oscillations in the T2K experiment using a \emphnew reconstruction algorithm for the far detector events. The improvements to the event selection brought by the adoption of the new algorithm allowed for an effective \emphincrease in statistics of around 30%. Due to my \emphcentral role in the implementation of the new analysis methods, I was selected for the paper editing committee and wrote the sections related to the far detector.
    DOI arXiv
  2. Hyper-Kamiokande Design Report
    K. Abe and others
    Mar 2018
    \footnotesize \\ – I led the development of the event reconstruction algorithm which allowed for the first time to realistically estimate the Physics performance of a water Cherenkov detector several times larger than Super-Kamiokande and thus \emphvalidate the design of the Hyper-Kamiokande experiment. I wrote the section of the design report describing this effort.
    arXiv

2016

  1. Measurement of the double-beta decay half-life and search for the neutrinoless double-beta decay of ^48Ca with the NEMO-3 detector
    R. Arnold and others
    Phys. Rev., Mar 2016
    \footnotesize \\ – This publication describes the main results I achieved during my PhD. I led the analysis which demonstrated that background-free searches for the neutrinoless double-beta decay of ^48Ca are possible up to large exposures, and produced the world’s most precise measurement to date of the two-neutrino double-beta decay of the same isotope. I co-authored the manuscript with my PhD supervisor.
    DOI arXiv
